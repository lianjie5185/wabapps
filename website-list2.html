Bilingua

Bilingua is your language exchange & learning companion. It connects you with other users who are fluent in a language you are learning, and makes sure to match you based on similar interests or personalities. Find the best conversation exchange partner so you can practice smart. The breakthrough language app provides guidance throughout the learning process and provides needed motivation with advanced gamification to help users along their language learning journey.

Babbel

If you only want to learn the basics of a language, try Babbel. This service offers 14 languages. These include English, French, German, Portuguese, Swedish and Turkish. With Babbel, you learn how to speak, read and write in your new language. Babbel formed in 2007 and received massive attention in Europe. The website and app are beginning to gain popularity in the United States. Because of its massive European following, Babbel is the top-grossing language learning app. The reason why it’s recommended for basic language learning is that the basic course is the only free course. If you enjoy Babbel enough to learn the advanced language, you’ll pay for the whole year. Prices start at $6.95.

Study now!

Cost:Free or $6.5+/year

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐



Learn a Language

Would you rather know the basics of a language? If you’re visiting another country temporarily but don’t wish to be fluent, there are simple language learning platforms such as Learn a Language. This website was actually created by the U.S. Institute of Languages. The goal of this website is to bridge communication gaps. The Learn a Language website also breaks up the different language learning structures. You have options to view words, phrases, and verbs in another language. They also offer full courses. The languages this website offers include French, Spanish, Italian, Japanese, German, Chinese, Russian, English, Arabic, Portuguese, Swedish, Hebrew, Greek, Turkish, Norwegian, Danish, Korean, Dutch, and even Latin.

Study now!

Cost:Free

Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐



Mangolanguages

Mango Languages was the first film-based language learning software. Today, Mango Languages offers courses on their website and mobile app. To access their website and app, you’ll have to pay $20 a month. But if you have a library card, you’re in luck. You can access Mango Languages free at any library in the United States and Canada. But get ready to get your butt kicked. Mango Languages isn’t easy and doesn’t offer an engaging platform like its competitors. Mango Languages offers 60-lesson courses for a myriad of different languages. Some of the languages they offer even include Swahili, Punjabi, and Icelandic.

Study now!

Cost:$20/month or Free
